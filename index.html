Chameleon:

  • Chameleons are reptiles and belong to the iguana suborder.
  • They change their body color in order to communicate and regulate body temperature.
  • Chameleon eyes have a 360-degree arc of vision and can see two directions at once.

Camel:

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Pigeon:

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Teddy-bear:

  • The incident of Roosevelt sparing the bear led to the popularity and naming of Teddy bears.
  • Modern teddy bears tend to have larger eyes and foreheads and smaller noses.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Panda:

  • Pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • An adult Panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.

Cicada:

  • Cicadas use straw-like mouth to extract the fluid from the plant's tissue.
  • Cicadas spend most of their life under the ground.
  • Cicadas are one of the loudest insects on the planet.
